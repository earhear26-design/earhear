Frequently Asked Questions

If you find yourself frequently asking people to repeat themselves, turning up the TV volume, or having difficulty hearing in noisy environments, it's time for a hearing test. Other signs include ringing in your ears (tinnitus), difficulty hearing high-pitched sounds, or feeling that others are mumbling.

Coverage varies by insurance provider and policy. Many health insurance plans offer partial coverage for hearing aids. We recommend checking with your insurance provider. We also offer flexible payment options and financing plans to make hearing care accessible.

With proper care and maintenance, hearing aids typically last 5-7 years. Factors affecting lifespan include usage patterns, maintenance, technological advancements, and individual ear conditions. Regular check-ups and cleaning can extend their life.

Digital hearing aids convert sound waves into digital signals, offering superior sound quality, noise reduction, and customization. They can distinguish between speech and background noise. Analog hearing aids simply amplify all sounds equally. We primarily recommend digital hearing aids for their advanced features and better performance.

Treatment duration varies based on the condition and individual progress. Most patients see improvement within 3-6 months with regular sessions. Our therapists create personalized treatment plans and adjust them based on your progress.
